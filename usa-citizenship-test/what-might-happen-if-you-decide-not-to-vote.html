What might happen if you decide not to vote?

(A) The government will force you to go and vote.

(B) You will be fined and face possible jail time.

(C) ** You’ll give up your say in how government is run.

(D) Your citizenship will be taken away for one year.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Not voting because of obligations related to everyday life made up 43% of all reasons reported by non-voters. This included being too busy (24%), having an illness or disability (11%), or being out of town (9%).

Concept note-2: -If no candidate receives a majority of electoral votes, the Presidential election leaves the Electoral College process and moves to Congress. The House of Representatives elects the President from the 3 Presidential candidates who received the most electoral votes.

Concept note-3: -"Get out the vote” or “getting out the vote” (GOTV) describes efforts aimed at increasing the voter turnout in elections. In countries that do not have or enforce compulsory voting, voter turnout can be low, sometimes even below a third of the eligible voter pool.
