USA CITIZENSHIP TEST

[SOURCES]
The citizenship exam test the person’s:

(A) ** ability to read, write, and speak English

(B) ability to serve in the armed forces

(C) understanding of traffic laws

(D) knowledge of Canadian history

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The naturalization test consists of two components: English language proficiency, which is determined by the applicant’s ability to read, write, speak and understand English; and. Knowledge of U.S. history and government, which is determined by a civics test.

Concept note-2: -Must be able to read, write and speak basic English. Some people do not have to speak English at all or answer any questions about United States history and civics, depending on many factors such as their age, time in the U.S. as green card holders, and if they have a disability.

Concept note-3: -Unless you qualify for a language accommodation, USCIS will administer the test in English. Even if you’re exempt from the English test, you will need to take the civics test in the language of your choice or qualify for an N-648 waiver. You may have heard that the Trump administration updated the civics test in 2020.

Concept note-4: -Posted by Frank Gogol in Immigrants | Updated on August 24, 2022. To obtain American citizenship, you need to get naturalized first. As part of the naturalization process, you must take a citizenship test in English.
