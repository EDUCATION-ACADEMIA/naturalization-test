This word means, “something you must do.”

(A) ** Duty/ obligation

(B) Responsibility

(C) Citizenship

(D) Civic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Some common synonyms of duty are assignment, chore, job, stint, and task. While all these words mean “a piece of work to be done, ” duty implies an obligation to perform or responsibility for performance.

Concept note-2: -accountability, agreement, bond, burden, commitment, constraint, contract, debt, duty, liability, necessity, need, promise, requirement, right, trust, understanding, business, call, cause.

Concept note-3: -An obligation is what one is bound to do to fulfill the dictates of usage, custom, or propriety, and to carry out a particular, specific, and often personal promise or agreement: financial obligations.

Concept note-4: -The generally accepted meanings of these two above words are as follows: duty is an obligation that one has to fulfill and responsibility is the act of accepting and acting on a task that has been assigned to you. However, in English grammar, the words ‘duty’ and ‘responsibility’ are often used interchangeably.
