Obeying laws:Duty or Responsibility?

(A) ** Duty

(B) Responsibilty

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Political obligation thus refers to the moral duty of citizens to obey the laws of their state. In cases where an act or forbearance that is required by law is morally obligatory on independent grounds, political obligation simply gives the citizen an additional reason for acting accordingly.

Concept note-2: -The duty to obey the law is a duty to conform one’s behavior to the rules of a particular legal system. Political obligation is the duty to obey (and perhaps support and defend) the state in which one holds citizenship.

Concept note-3: -In order to obey the law a person must act with some recognition that she is in fact doing an action that the law requires. In a phrase, obedience requires knowingly doing the right thing.

Concept note-4: -406–7). Gilbert aside, theorists of political obligation characterize it as a moral duty to obey the law. As such, it provides a person with a categorical reason for action, one that does not depend on her inclinations or self-interest.

Concept note-5: -Some legal philosophers argue that there a general moral obligation to obey the law-that the fact that a law demands certain behaviour should be morally important to us regardless of what the law actually says.
