The stripes on the flag represent:

(A) number of states

(B) ** original states

(C) Signers of the Declaration of Independence

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are 50 stars representing the 50 states and there are 13 stripes representing the 13 original colonies.

Concept note-2: -The flag of the United States of America is a national flag. It has 7 red stripes and 6 white stripes. These 13 stripes represent the original thirteen colonies. The flag also has a blue canton, a rectangle in the top left corner.

Concept note-3: -Today the flag consists of 13 horizontal stripes, seven red alternating with six white. The stripes represent the original 13 Colonies and the stars represent the 50 states of the Union.

Concept note-4: -On June 14, 1777, in order to establish an official flag of the new nation, the Continental Congress passed the first Flag Act: â€œResolved, that the flag of the thirteen United States be thirteen stripes alternate red and white; that the union be thirteen stars, white in a blue field, representing a new constellation."

Concept note-5: -The American flag remains a living piece of history and a source of pride and unity for all Americans. The Stars and Stripes embodies the very qualities that make our nation great: liberty, justice, freedom, love of country and national purpose.
