TRUE or FALSE. A naturalized citizen has the same rights as someone who was born a U.S. citizen.

(A) ** TRUE

(B) FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The main difference between naturalization and citizenship is that a citizenship certificate is given to someone who acquires citizenship through their US parents by being born in the country. A certificate of naturalization is obtained by someone who becomes a citizen through the naturalization process.

Concept note-2: -Overview. A natural-born citizen refers to someone who was a U.S. citizen at birth, and did not need to go through a naturalization proceeding later in life.

Concept note-3: -What is the difference between a natural born and a naturalized citizen? A natural born citizen is a person born in the USA to 2 legal American citizens at the time of the citizen’s birth. A naturalized citizen is an immigrant that goes through the process to become a legal citizen and takes that oath.

Concept note-4: -A newborn baby receives U.S. citizenship automatically, but it doesn’t receive all related documents immediately. Parents must formalize documents and receive a birth certificate before leaving the hospital.
