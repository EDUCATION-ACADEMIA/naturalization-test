The study of citizenship and government is known as

(A) Citizenship Development

(B) ** Civics

(C) Values

(D) Government

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Civic education is the study of the theoretical, political and practical aspects of citizenship.

Concept note-2: -Civics is the study of the rights and obligations of citizens in society. The term derives from the Latin word civicus, meaning “relating to a citizen".

Concept note-3: -Civics is the study of government. It most often refers to studying government in high school to prepare to be a good citizen. In college, civics is usually called political science.

Concept note-4: -Civics is defined as: the study or science of the privileges and obligations of citizens. Civic education is the study of the theoretical, political and practical aspects of citizenship, as well as its rights and duties.

Concept note-5: -The study of citizenship is called civics. If your school offers a civics class, you can learn about the importance of things like voting and paying taxes. Student government can teach you about civics, and so can certain social studies lessons and classes that a country’s new citizens sometimes take.
