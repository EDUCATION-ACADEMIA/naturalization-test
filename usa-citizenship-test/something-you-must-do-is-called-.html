[SOURCES]
Something you MUST do is called ____

(A) a responsibility

(B) a right

(C) ** a duty

(D) nothing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -obligation. noun. something that you must do for legal or moral reasons.

Concept note-2: -An obligation is what one is bound to do to fulfill the dictates of usage, custom, or propriety, and to carry out a particular, specific, and often personal promise or agreement: financial obligations.

Concept note-3: -Some common synonyms of duty are assignment, chore, job, stint, and task. While all these words mean “a piece of work to be done, ” duty implies an obligation to perform or responsibility for performance.

Concept note-4: -/ˌkɔːl əv ˈdjuː.ti/ the feeling that you should do something because it is your duty: For Anne, the call of duty was strong. These heroes courageously answered the call of duty.

Concept note-5: -duty noun [C or U] (RESPONSIBILITY) something that you have to do because it is part of your job, or something that you feel is the right thing to do: The duty of the agency is to act in the best interests of the child. [ + to infinitive ] I felt it was my duty to tell them the truth.
