Citizens must

(A) ** obey the law

(B) ignore the law

(C) break the law

(D) make the law

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Citizens are supposed to obey the law, participate in the democratic process, and act for the welfare of the community. Both rights and duties are essential components of a healthy relationship between the citizens and the state. With the rise of democracy, the concept of citizenship has gained importance.

Concept note-2: -All legal systems claim to bind people subject to them; part of what we mean by a valid law is that the relevant population is required to obey it. This requirement is generally supported by coercion, while those who do not obey are subject to sanctions.

Concept note-3: -In order to obey the law a person must act with some recognition that she is in fact doing an action that the law requires. In a phrase, obedience requires knowingly doing the right thing.

Concept note-4: -because it is our RESPONSIBLITY and also we have to follow the rules and regulations to live independently . People also obey the law because they respect authority. Most of these laws are in effect to protect us.
