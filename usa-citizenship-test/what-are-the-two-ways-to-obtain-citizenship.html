What are the TWO ways to obtain citizenship?

(A) ** Birth and Naturalization

(B) Birth and Purchase

(C) Naturalization and Immigration

(D) Birth and Adoption

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: There are three ways of acquiring citizenship namely, birth (Section 3), registration (Section 5), descent (Section 4), naturalisation (Section 6), and incorporation of territory (Section 7).

Concept note-2: -There are two kinds of citizenships in India. One is ‘natural’-by birth and the other is ‘acquired’ citizenship. If you’re born in India and your parents are Indian citizens, then you’re an Indian citizen. The other is ‘acquired’ wherein people can acquire Indian citizenship on the basis of certain rules.

Concept note-3: -The person must have resided (lived) in India for 11 years in the previous 14 years and one year immediately before the time of making the application. Note: After the Citizenship Amendment Act 2019, the number of years from eleven has been decreased to five years and one year immediately before making the application.

Concept note-4: -Becoming a citizen through naturalization is a process in which a non-U.S. citizen voluntarily becomes an American citizen. U.S. citizens: Owe their allegiance to the United States. Are entitled to its protection.
