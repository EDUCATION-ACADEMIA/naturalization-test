Under our constitution, some powers belong to the federal government, what is one of those powers?

(A) Choose the Vice President

(B) Set election days

(C) ** To declare war

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is one power of the federal government? The powers of the United States government are listed in the Constitution. It has the power to declare war. The president can ask Congress for a declaration of war.

Concept note-2: -These included: to lay and collect taxes; pay debts and borrow money; regulate commerce; coin money; establish post offices; protect patents and copyrights; establish lower courts; declare war; and raise and support an Army and Navy.

Concept note-3: -Only the federal government can coin money, regulate the mail, declare war, or conduct foreign affairs.

Concept note-4: -3 Declarations of War. Article I, Section 8, Clause 11: [The Congress shall have Power . . . ] To declare War, grant Letters of Marque and Reprisal, and make Rules concerning Captures on Land and Water; . . .
