The U.S. population tripled between 1790 and 1830 primarily because of

(A) ** high birthrate

(B) a mistake in the census

(C) the low death rate

(D) immigration

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Between 1860 and 1910, the population of the United States nearly tripled. This population growth provided industry with a large workforce and created greater demand for consumer goods. Population growth stemmed from two sources-large families and a flood of immigrants.

Concept note-2: -Between 1820 and 1840, the American population increased about 7 milion from 10 million to 17 million.

Concept note-3: -From 1960 to 2021 the population of United States of America increased from 180.67 million to 331.89 million people. This is a growth of 83.7 percent in 61 years. The highest increase in the United States was recorded in 1961 with 1.67 percent. The smallest increase in 2021 with 0.12 percent.

Concept note-4: -What was the biggest single reason for the american population growth in the nineteenth century. 1. The biggest reason for American population growth was immigration.
