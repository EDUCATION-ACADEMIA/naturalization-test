Which of the following amendments describe a citizen’s right to vote?

(A) 2

(B) 5

(C) 8

(D) ** 15

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The right of citizens of the United States to vote shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or by any State on account of race, color, or previous condition of ser-vitude. SECTION 2.

Concept note-2: -The Fourteenth Amendment also added the first mention of gender into the Constitution. It declared that all male citizens over twenty-one years old should be able to vote. In 1870, the Fifteenth Amendment affirmed that the right to vote “shall not be denied…on account of race.”

Concept note-3: -Passed by Congress February 26, 1869, and ratified February 3, 1870, the 15th Amendment granted African American men the right to vote.

Concept note-4: -Civil War Amendments (Thirteenth, Fourteenth, and Fifteenth Amendments) | Constitution Annotated | Congress.gov | Library of Congress. Browse Explanations.

Concept note-5: -The Constitution (15th Amendment Act), 1963 Provided that retired high court judges are appointed as acting judges of the same court. Provided the compensatory allowance for the transfer of judges from one High Court to another.
