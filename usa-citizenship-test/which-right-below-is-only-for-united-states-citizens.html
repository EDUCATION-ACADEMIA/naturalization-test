Which right below is only for United States citizens?

(A) attend public school

(B) freedom of religion

(C) freedom of speech

(D) ** run for public office

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Citizens vote for leaders to represent them and their ideas, and the leaders support the citizens’ interests. There are two special rights only for U.S. citizens: voting in federal elections and running for federal office.

Concept note-2: -Sections. “No Person shall be a Representative who shall not have attained to the age of twenty five Years, and been seven Years a Citizen of the United States, and who shall not, when elected, be an Inhabitant of that State in which he shall be chosen.”

Concept note-3: -What is a requirement of all citizens in the United States? Everyone must register with the Selective Service System. Everyone must be available to serve on a jury in response to a summons. Everyone must cast a vote in all municipal, state, and federal elections.

Concept note-4: -Common Political Rights are: Right to Vote. Right to be Elected. Right to Hold Public Offices.

Concept note-5: -The right to vote in the Constitution of India is guaranteed under Article 326.
