What is the minimum age to be the president?

(A) 25

(B) 30

(C) ** 35

(D) 40

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -According to Article 58 of the Constitution, no person shall be eligible for election as President unless he is a citizen of India, has completed the age of thirty-five years and is qualified for election as a member of the House of the People.

Concept note-2: -The president selection process: The Constitution only lists three-term presidential qualifications-the president must be 35 years old, a natural-born citizen, and must have lived in the United States for at least 14 years.

Concept note-3: -Article 58 of the constitution lays out the basic requirements for running for president. A president must be an Indian citizen. He must be at least 35 years old. For the president of India, there is no upper age limit.
