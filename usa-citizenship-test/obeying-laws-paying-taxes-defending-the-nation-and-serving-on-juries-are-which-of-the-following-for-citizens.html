Obeying laws, paying taxes, defending the nation and serving on juries are which of the following for citizens?

(A) rights

(B) ** obligations

(C) freedoms

(D) rules

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A duty (also called an obligation) is something that a citizen is required to do, by law. Examples of duties/obligations are: obeying laws, paying taxes, defending the nation and serving on juries.

Concept note-2: -As per the Direct taxation laws; income and other kinds of taxes are a legal obligation for an Indian citizen as he uses public services, receives social and political security from the state etc. Q.

Concept note-3: -1)To abide by the Constitution and respect its ideals and institutions, the National Flag and the National Anthem. 2)To cherish and follow the noble ideals which inspired our national struggle for freedom. 3)To uphold and protect the sovereignty, unity and integrity of India.

Concept note-4: -Active participation comes in many different forms that include both obligations and responsibilities. Obeying the laws, registering for selective service, defending our nation, serving on juries, and paying taxes are all obligations as well as examples of active participation in the society and government.

Concept note-5: -An obligation is an action that citizens do that is required by law. Responsibilities are actions citizens do for the common good. Some examples of responsibilities include voting, community service, attending civic meetings, and petitioning the government.
