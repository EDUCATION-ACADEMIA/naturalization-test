Which type of landmark is Mount Rushmore?

(A) Physical

(B) ** Cultural

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Per its National Register nomination, the Mount Rushmore cultural landscape remains significant for its ability to yield prehistoric information, artistic value, and association with historic figures and events. The period of significance stretches from 3500 BC to 1967 AD.

Concept note-2: -Mount Rushmore National Memorial is a national memorial centered on a colossal sculpture carved into the granite face of Mount Rushmore (Lakota: Tȟuŋkášila Šákpe, or Six Grandfathers) in the Black Hills near Keystone, South Dakota, United States.

Concept note-3: -"The purpose of the memorial is to communicate the founding, expansion, preservation, and unification of the United States with colossal statues of Washington, Jefferson, Lincoln, and Theodore Roosevelt."

Concept note-4: -Mount Rushmore, also known as the Shrine of Democracy, is a National Monument and Memorial depicting four of the most prominent presidents of the first 150 years of the United States – George Washington, Abraham Lincoln, Teddy Roosevelt, and Thomas Jefferson.
