If you wanted independence from Great Britain, you would be labeled a

(A) ** Patriot

(B) Loyalist

(C) American

(D) Undecided

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Those who favored independence from Great Britain were called Patriots . Those who wished that the Colonies remain tied to Great Britain were known as Loyalists . Americans who elected not to choose a side were called Neutrals.

Concept note-2: -Most Patriots supported independence because they felt that recent British laws on the American Colonies violated their rights as British citizens (e.g. taxing without consent, quartering soldiers in citizens’ homes, and denying colonists the right to a trial).

Concept note-3: -Patriots wanted the Thirteen colonies to gain independence from Britain. They wanted to create their own laws and to form the United States of America. The Patriots wanted freedom from British rule because they didn’t think they were treated well.

Concept note-4: -Patriots, also known as Whigs, were the colonists who rebelled against British monarchial control. Their rebellion was based on the social and political philosophy of republicanism, which rejected the ideas of a monarchy and aristocracy – essentially, inherited power.

Concept note-5: -Patriots, also known as Revolutionaries, Continentals, Rebels, or American Whigs, were the colonists of the Thirteen Colonies who rejected British rule over the colonies.
