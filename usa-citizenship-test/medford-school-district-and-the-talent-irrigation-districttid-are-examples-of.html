Medford School District and the Talent Irrigation District(TID) are examples of:

(A) Counties

(B) Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs)

(C) Transportation Districts

(D) ** Special Districts

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Special districts are limited purpose local governments – separate from cities and counties. Within their boundaries, special districts provide focused public services such as fire protection, sewers, water supply, electricity, parks, recreation, sanitation, cemeteries, and libraries.

Concept note-2: -That would be the fire protection district, according to the 2012 Census of Governments-though it really depends on how you slice it. That’s just the most common single type of special district.

Concept note-3: -Single-function special districts are the most common type, examples of which include school building authorities, libraries, hospitals, health, highways, air transportation, fire protection, drainage or flood control, irrigation, sewerage, solid-waste management, water supply, cemeteries, and mosquito abatement.

Concept note-4: -Examples include Kern County Water Agency, Fairfield-Suisun Sewer District, and Humboldt Bay Harbor, Recreation, and Conservation District.
