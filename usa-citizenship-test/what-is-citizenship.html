What is citizenship?

(A) Something that is given automatically when a person moves to a different country

(B) ** Being a member of a nation or country and having full rights and responsibilities under the law.

(C) That other grade on your report card that nobody really looks at

(D) A large boat that only carries passengers that are members of a certain country

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Citizenship implies the status of freedom with accompanying responsibilities. Citizens have certain rights, duties, and responsibilities that are denied or only partially extended to aliens and other noncitizens residing in a country.

Concept note-2: -Citizenship is the status of a person recognized under law as being a legal member of a sovereign state or belonging to a nation. In India, Articles 5 – 11 of the Constitution deals with the concept of citizenship.

Concept note-3: -A citizen is a participatory member of a political community. Citizenship is gained by meeting the legal requirements of a national, state, or local government. A nation grants certain rights and privileges to its citizens. In return, citizens are expected to obey their country’s laws and defend it against its enemies.

Concept note-4: -Article Five: Citizenship During the Commencement of the Constitution. (c) An individual who has been ordinarily resident in the Indian territory for at least 5 years immediately preceding such commencement, shall be a citizen of India.

Concept note-5: -Citizenship refers to a person’s full membership in any state in which he or she possesses civil and political rights. Citizenship is a bond between an individual and a country to which the individual owes allegiance and is entitled to protection in return.
