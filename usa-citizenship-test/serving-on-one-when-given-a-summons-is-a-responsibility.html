Serving on one when given a summons is a responsibility

(A) ** Jury

(B) Senate

(C) Council

(D) Miltary

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Every summon shall be served by a police officer, or by an officer of the court issuing, it or any other public servant. The summon shall, if practical, be served personally on the person summoned, by delivering or tendering to him one of the duplicates of the summons.

Concept note-2: -(1) When a suit has been duly instituted a summons shall be issued to the defendant to appear and answer the claim. (2) Every summons shall be signed by the Registrar, and shall be sealed with the seal of the Court. (3) Every summons shall be accompanied by a copy of the plaint.

Concept note-3: -Summons issued by the court personally to the Defendant or his agent. After such service the officer of the court (Belief of court) shall obtain signature of the Defendant or his agent or adult member of his family to whom served personally and return original copy of summons to the court with his report.
