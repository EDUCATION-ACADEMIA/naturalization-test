History:Who was president during the Great Depression and World War II?

(A) Richard Nixon

(B) Andrew Jackson

(C) George Washington

(D) ** Franklin D. Roosevelt

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Roosevelt directed the federal government during most of the Great Depression, implementing his New Deal domestic agenda in response to the worst economic crisis in U.S. history.

Concept note-2: -The biography for President Roosevelt and past presidents is courtesy of the White House Historical Association. Assuming the Presidency at the depth of the Great Depression, Franklin D. Roosevelt helped the American people regain faith in themselves.

Concept note-3: -Before serving as America’s 31st President from 1929 to 1933, Herbert Hoover had achieved international success as a mining engineer and worldwide gratitude as “The Great Humanitarian” who fed war-torn Europe during and after World War I.

Concept note-4: -After leading the United States through nearly a decade of Depression, President Franklin Delano Roosevelt took on the role of Commander-in-Chief when the United States entered the Second World War.

Concept note-5: -President Franklin Roosevelt [FDR]: One of our greatest Presidents [1932-1945], FDR creates the New Deal to help the nation through the Great Depression of the 1930s, and he also leads the nation through WWII 1941-1945.
