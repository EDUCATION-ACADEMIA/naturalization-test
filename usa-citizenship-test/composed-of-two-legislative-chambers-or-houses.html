Composed of two legislative chambers or houses.

(A) ** Bicameral

(B) Balanced Budget

(C) Bill of Rights

(D) Municipal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A bicameral legislature is a legislature with two houses. The Indian Parliament consists of two houses-Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha. Few states in India also have bicameral legislature consisting of Vidhan Sabha and Vidhan Parishad, while others have a single house, i.e. Vidhan Sabha.

Concept note-2: -The Parliament of India is bicameral or bicameral legislature which consists of two houses-the upper house (Rajya Sabha) and the lower house (Lok Sabha) along with the president of India.

Concept note-3: -In our country, the Parliament consists of two Houses. The two Houses are known as the Council of States (Rajya Sabha) and the House of the People (Lok Sabha). The President of India is a part of the Parliament, although she is not a member of either House.

Concept note-4: -Legislature of the Union, which is called Parliament, consists of the President and two Houses, known as Council of States (Rajya Sabha) and House of the People (Lok Sabha).
