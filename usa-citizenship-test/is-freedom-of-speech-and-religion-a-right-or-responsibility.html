Is freedom of speech and religion a right or responsibility?

(A) ** Right

(B) Responsibility

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Yes, freedom of speech is a fundamental right guaranteed under Article 19(1)(a). What is Article 19 of the Constitution of India? Article 19 of the Constitution guaranteed the right to freedom.

Concept note-2: -Scope of the Right The right to freedom of expression comes with responsibilities. The Government can lawfully restrict this right if the restriction is necessary to protect the rights of others or to protect public order, public health, public morality or national security.

Concept note-3: -For our freedom of speech to work-to have meaning or the power to improve our democracy-we need to listen to one another. In fact, we have a responsibility to listen, because listening allows us to extend the freedom of speech to others.

Concept note-4: -The Bill of Rights guarantees that the government can never deprive people in the U.S. of certain fundamental rights including the right to freedom of religion and to free speech and the due process of law.

Concept note-5: -Article 25 gives every person the right to freedom of conscience and the right to freely profess, practice, and propagate religion subject to public order, morality, and health. Article 26 also gives all denominations the right to manage their own affairs in matters of religion.
