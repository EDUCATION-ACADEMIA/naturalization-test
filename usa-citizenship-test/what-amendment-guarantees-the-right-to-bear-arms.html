What amendment guarantees the right to bear arms?

(A) 1st amendment

(B) ** 2nd amendment

(C) 5th amendment

(D) 14th amendment

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A well regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed.

Concept note-2: -THE CONSTITUTION (SECOND AMENDMENT) ACT, 1952. Statement of Objects and Reasons appended to THE CONSTITUTION (Second Amendment) Bill, 1952 which was enacted as the the Constitution (Second Amendment) Act, 1952. Article 81(1)(a) prescribes an absolute limit of 500 elected members in the House of the People.

Concept note-3: -The right to bear arms generally refers to a person’s right to possess weapons. Over the years, the Supreme Court has interpreted the Constitution’s right to bear arms as an individual self-defense right, making it very difficult for Congress to regulate guns.
