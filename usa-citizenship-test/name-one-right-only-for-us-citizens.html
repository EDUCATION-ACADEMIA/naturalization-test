Name one right only for U.S. citizens

(A) freedom of Religion

(B) Attend public school

(C) ** Run for federal office

(D) Freedom of speech

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Only U.S. citizens can vote in federal elections. Non-citizens may only vote in some local elections.

Concept note-2: -Voting. Only U.S. citizens can vote in Federal elections. Most States also restrict the right to vote, in most elections, to U.S. citizens.

Concept note-3: -The Bill of Rights protects freedom of speech, freedom of religion, the right to keep and bear arms, the freedom of assembly and the freedom to petition. It also prohibits unreasonable search and seizure, cruel and unusual punishment and compelled self-incrimination.
