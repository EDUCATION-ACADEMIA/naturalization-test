Which is the highest court in the United States?

(A) The Pennsylvania Supreme Court

(B) ** The US Supreme Court

(C) The Presidentâ€™s Special Court

(D) The County Courts

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Supreme Court of the United States is the highest court in the land and the only part of the federal judiciary specifically required by the Constitution.

Concept note-2: -The Supreme Court is the highest court in the United States. Article III of the U.S. Constitution created the Supreme Court and authorized Congress to pass laws establishing a system of lower courts.

Concept note-3: -The federal court system has three main levels: district courts (the trial court), circuit courts which are the first level of appeal, and the Supreme Court of the United States, the final level of appeal in the federal system.

Concept note-4: -First, as the highest court in the land, it is the court of last resort for those looking for justice. Second, due to its power of judicial review, it plays an essential role in ensuring that each branch of government recognizes the limits of its own power.

Concept note-5: -As the final arbiter of the law, the Court is charged with ensuring the American people the promise of equal justice under law and, thereby, also functions as guardian and interpreter of the Constitution.
