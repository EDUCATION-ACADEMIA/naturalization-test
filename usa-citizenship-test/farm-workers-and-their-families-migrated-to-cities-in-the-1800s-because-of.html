Farm workers and their families migrated to cities in the 1800s because of

(A) ** new factory jobs

(B) overcrowding

(C) automobiles

(D) better schools

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -“Cities grew because industrial factories required large workforces and workers and their families needed places to live near their jobs. Factories and cities attracted millions of immigrants looking for work and a better life in the United States.”

Concept note-2: -By the late 1700s many people could no longer earn their living in the countryside. Increasingly, people moved from farms and villages into bigger towns and cities to find work in factories. Cities grew larger, but they were often dirty, crowded, and unhealthy. Machines greatly increased production.

Concept note-3: -Many left their agrarian lives behind and headed for towns and cities to find employment. Advances in industry and the growth of factory production accelerated the trend toward urbanization in Britain.

Concept note-4: -The principal force driving America’s move into cities was the Second Industrial Revolution.
