The king’s “Stamp Act” forced colonists to pay taxes on ____

(A) Pizza and Spaghetti

(B) ** Printed Documents and Legal Material

(C) Instruments and Tea

(D) Lumber and Lead

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -11) On March 22, 1765, the British Parliament passed the “Stamp Act” to help pay for British troops stationed in the colonies during the Seven Years’ War. The act required the colonists to pay a tax, represented by a stamp, on various forms of papers, documents, and playing cards.

Concept note-2: -The Stamp Act of 1765 was ratified by the British parliament under King George III. It imposed a tax on all papers and official documents in the American colonies, though not in England.

Concept note-3: -Stamp Act. Parliament’s first direct tax on the American colonies, this act, like those passed in 1764, was enacted to raise money for Britain. It taxed newspapers, almanacs, pamphlets, broadsides, legal documents, dice, and playing cards.

Concept note-4: -The British needed to station a large army in North America as a consequence and on 22 March 1765 the British Parliament passed the Stamp Act, which sought to raise money to pay for this army through a tax on all legal and official papers and publications circulating in the colonies.

Concept note-5: -British Parliament repealed the Stamp Act in 1766, primarily as a result of petitioning by British merchants whose export businesses from the colonies were hurt by colonial response.
