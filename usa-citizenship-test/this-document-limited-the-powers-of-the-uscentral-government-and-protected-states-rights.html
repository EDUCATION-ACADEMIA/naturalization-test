This document limited the powers of the U.S.central government and protected states rights.

(A) ** Articles of Confederation

(B) Magna Carta

(C) Bill of Rights

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Articles of Confederation served as the written document that established the functions of the national government of the United States after it declared independence from Great Britain.

Concept note-2: -The Articles of Confederation were adopted by the Continental Congress on November 15, 1777. This document served as the United States’ first constitution. It was in force from March 1, 1781, until 1789 when the present-day Constitution went into effect.

Concept note-3: -ARTICLE II. Each State retains its Sovereignty, freedom and independence, and every Power, Jurisdiction and right, which is not by this confederation expressly dele-gated to the United States in Congress assembled.

Concept note-4: -The first ten amendments to the Constitution are known as the Bill of Rights and were ratified in 1791. These amendments limited federal power, by protecting both the rights of individuals and the authority of the states.

Concept note-5: -In order to protect states’ rights, the Articles set strict limits on congressional authority. Under the Articles, the states, not Congress had the power to tax. Congress could raise money only by asking the states for funds, borrowing from foreign governments, and selling western lands.
