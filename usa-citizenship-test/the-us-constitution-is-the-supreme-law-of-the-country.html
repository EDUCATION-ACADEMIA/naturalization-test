The U.S. constitution is the supreme law of the country.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Solution: The above statement is True. The Constitution is the supreme law of a country. The Constitution and its principles govern the functioning of a country.

Concept note-2: -The fountain source of law in India is the Constitution which, in turn, gives due recognition to statutes, case law and customary law consistent with its dispensations. Statutes are enacted by Parliament, State Legislatures and Union Territory Legislatures.

Concept note-3: -The Constitution is the Fundamental Law of the Land and it is the source of all the Laws in a Country.

Concept note-4: -The U.S. Constitution is the nation’s fundamental law. It codifies the core values of the people. Courts have the responsibility to interpret the Constitution’s meaning, as well as the meaning of any laws passed by Congress.
