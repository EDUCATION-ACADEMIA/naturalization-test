The First Amendment says the government cannot tell media companies, like newspapers, online news outlets, and television stations what to report. This is freedom of

(A) the press

(B) ** assembly

(C) religion

(D) speech

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The First Amendment protects individuals from government censorship. Social media platforms are private companies and can censor what people post on their websites as they see fit.

Concept note-2: -Ruling unanimously in Reno v. ACLU, the Court declared the Internet to be a free speech zone, deserving of at least as much First Amendment protection as that afforded to books, newspapers and magazines.
