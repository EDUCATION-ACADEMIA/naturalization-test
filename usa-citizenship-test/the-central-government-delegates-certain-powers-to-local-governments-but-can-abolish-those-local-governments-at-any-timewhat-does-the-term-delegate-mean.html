The central government delegates certain powers to local governments, but can abolish those local governments at any time.What does the term delegate mean?

(A) ** Assign

(B) Take

(C) Separates

(D) Cancels

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -CONTROL OF DELEGATED LEGISLATION Committee on statutory instrument. Judicial or legal or court control. Ministerial control. Ministerial accountability.

Concept note-2: -Delegated legislation is a variant of rule making, and it has been the practice of Law making bodies to delegate law making duties to legislative organs. The law emanating from such delegation of powers is called delegated legislation.

Concept note-3: -Delegated (also known as subordinate) legislation is legislation made not directly by an Act of the Parliament, but under the authority of an Act of the Parliament. Parliament has regularly and extensively delegated to the Executive Government limited power to make certain regulations under Acts.

Concept note-4: -delegation of powers, in U.S. constitutional law, the transfer of a specific authority by one of the three branches of government (executive, legislative, and judicial) to another branch or to an independent agency.
