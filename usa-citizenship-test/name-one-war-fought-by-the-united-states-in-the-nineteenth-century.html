Name one war fought by the United States in the nineteenth century.

(A) ** Mexican-American War

(B) World War I

(C) World War II

(D) Korean War

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The United States fought four wars in the 1800s: the War of 1812, the Mexican-American War, the Civil War, and the Spanish-American War. The United States fought Mexico in the Mexican-American War from 1846 to 1848. The United States wanted to expand west.

Concept note-2: -The Civil War was the biggest U.S. war in the 1800s. The Civil War was between the North and the South. This war was from 1861 to 1865. Many Northern and Southern soldiers died in the war.

Concept note-3: -The Mexican-American War was a conflict between the United States and Mexico, fought from April 1846 to February 1848.

Concept note-4: -Courtesy of the Library of Congress, LC-USZC4-6200. The Mexican-American War was between the United States and Mexico. This war was from 1846 to 1848.

Concept note-5: -The Mexican-American War Begins Taylor called in reinforcements, and-with the help of superior rifles and artillery-was able to defeat the Mexicans at the Battle of Palo Alto and the Battle of Resaca de la Palma.
