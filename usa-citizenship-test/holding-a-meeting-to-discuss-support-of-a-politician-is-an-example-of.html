Holding a meeting to discuss support of a Politician is an example of:

(A) Freedom of Religion

(B) Freedom of the Press

(C) ** Freedom of Assembly

(D) Freedom of Petition

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The First Amendment prohibits government from abridging “the right of the people peaceably to assemble.” This basic freedom ensures that the spirit of the First Amendment survives and thrives even when the majority of citizens would rather suppress expression it finds offensive.

Concept note-2: -Such assemblies may take many forms, including demonstrations, protests, meetings, rallies, online assemblies, civil disobedience or direct action campaigns, provided that they are non-violent. Everyone has the right of peaceful assembly: including non-citizens and children.

Concept note-3: -Freedom of speech includes the right: Of students to wear black armbands to school to protest a war (“Students do not shed their constitutional rights at the schoolhouse gate.”). Tinker v. Des Moines, 393 U.S. 503 (1969). To use certain offensive words and phrases to convey political messages.

Concept note-4: -Freedom of expression includes the freedom to hold, share and explore opinions and ideas. This includes the right for individuals to express their views through public protest and demonstration, and so this right is closely related to the right to peaceful assembly.
