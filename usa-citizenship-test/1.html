Good government requires its citizens to be

(A) ** Educated and informed

(B) Powerful

(C) Wealthy

(D) Self-involved

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This paper suggests that good governance can serve as an entry point to raising institutional performance in the delivery of education services. Crucial to high performance are standards, information, incentives and accountability.

Concept note-2: -In summary, good governance relates to the political and institutional processes and outcomes that are necessary to achieve the goals of development. The true test of ‘good’ governance is the degree to which it delivers on the promise of human rights: civil, cultural, economic, political and social rights.

Concept note-3: -Accountability is a key requirement of good governance. Not only governmental institutions but also the private sector and civil society organizations must be accountable to the public and to their institutional stakeholders.
