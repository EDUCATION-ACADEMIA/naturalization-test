Concept note-1: -One of the core explanations for the existence of political parties is that they arise from pre-existing divisions among people: society is divided in a certain way, and a party is formed to organize that division into the electoral competition.

Concept note-2: -The election campaigns are conducted to have a free and open discussion about who is a better representative and in turn, which party will make a better government.

Concept note-3: -Elections can be a primary tool to foster political openings and expand political participation. Electoral processes offer political parties and civic groups an opportunity to mobilize and organize supporters and share alternative platforms with the public.

Concept note-4: -We need political parties because parties contest elections. Parties play a decisive role in making laws for a country. Parties form and run governments. Political parties play the role of opposition.
