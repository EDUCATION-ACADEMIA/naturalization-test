Which practice is part of the obligation of a U.S. citizen to help defend the nation?

(A) Serve on a jury

(B) ** Register for selective service

(C) Vote in all elections

(D) Keep informed about national issues

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Active participation comes in many different forms for both obligations and responsibilities. Obeying the laws, registering for selective service, defending our nation, serving on juries and paying taxes are all obligations and examples of active participation in the society and government.

Concept note-2: -All citizens have certain duties and this includes paying taxes and following traffic signals. As citizens we ought to curb social evils like gambling and dowry. These duties help us to lead better and responsible lives.

Concept note-3: -On May 18, 1917, Congress passed the Selective Service Act, which authorized the Federal Government to temporarily expand the military through conscription. The act eventually required all men between the ages of 21 to 45 to register for military service.
