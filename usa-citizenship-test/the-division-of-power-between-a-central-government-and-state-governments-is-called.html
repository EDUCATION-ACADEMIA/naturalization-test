The division of power between a central government and state governments is called

(A) The United States

(B) the Supremacy Clause

(C) the Necessary and Proper Clause

(D) ** federalism

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Federalism is a system of government in which the power is divided between a central authority and various constituent units of the country. Usually, a federation has two levels of government. One is the government for the entire country that is usually responsible for a few subjects of common national interest.

Concept note-2: -Federalism is the system of government in which power is divided between a central government and regional governments; in the United States, both the national government and the state governments possess a large measure of sovereignty.

Concept note-3: -Solution: Power division between Governments at the Union level and provincial or regional level is a federal division of power. It refers to the general government for the entire country and governments at the provincial or regional level. Such a system is called to be a federal system.

Concept note-4: -Federalism is the word used to describe the Constitutionâ€™s system of dividing political power between the national government and the states.

Concept note-5: -On the basis of relationship between the centre and the units, the governments may be classified as unitary and federal. In a unitary government, all the powers of government are vested in the central government whereas in a federal government, the powers of government are divided between the centre and the units.
