Who was the President during World War I?

(A) Warren Harding

(B) ** Woodrow Wilson

(C) Franklin Roosevelt

(D) Theodore Roosevelt

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Woodrow Wilson, a leader of the Progressive Movement, was the 28th President of the United States (1913-1921). After a policy of neutrality at the outbreak of World War I, Wilson led America into war in order to “make the world safe for democracy.”

Concept note-2: -Wilson tried to keep the United States neutral during World War I but ultimately called on Congress to declare war on Germany in 1917. After the war, he helped negotiate a peace treaty that included a plan for the League of Nations.
