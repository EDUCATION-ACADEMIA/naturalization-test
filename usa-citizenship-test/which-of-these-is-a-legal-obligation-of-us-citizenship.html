Which of these is a legal obligation of US citizenship?

(A) to reside in the US

(B) to save money for retirement

(C) to vote in Presidential elections

(D) ** to serve on a jury when summoned

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A prospective juror must: Be a citizen of the United States. Be a resident of the county in which they were summoned. Be eighteen years of age or older.

Concept note-2: -In order to be eligible to serve as a juror, a person must be at least 18 years of age or older, and a US citizen. Non-citizens (including green card holders, non-immigrants, and TNTs) would therefore not be eligible to serve as a juror.

Concept note-3: -U.S. citizens, 18-years-old and older, may qualify to serve in the federal courts on a jury. The Jury Selection and Service Act establishes the process for selecting jurors and outlines qualifications a person must meet to serve on a federal jury.

Concept note-4: -There are three groups that are exempt from federal jury service: members of the armed forces on active duty; members of professional fire and police departments; and. “public officers” of federal, state or local governments, who are actively engaged full-time in the performance of public duties.

Concept note-5: -Jury duty is your duty as an American to serve as a juror during a court proceeding. When you serve on a jury, you’re ensuring the defendant’s Sixth Amendment right to a speedy trial and an impartial jury. 1. If you are called for jury duty, you must appear before the court or risk being held in contempt of court.
