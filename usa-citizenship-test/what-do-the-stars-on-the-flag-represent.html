What do the stars on the flag represent?

(A) ** Number of states

(B) Number of presidents

(C) Number that looked nice

(D) original colonies

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are 50 stars representing the 50 states and there are 13 stripes representing the 13 original colonies. from The CIA World Factbook.

Concept note-2: -The stripes represent the original 13 Colonies and the stars represent the 50 states of the Union.

Concept note-3: -Act of April 4, 1818, provided for 13 stripes and one star for each state, to be added to the flag on the 4th of July following the admission of each new state.

Concept note-4: -The topmost star is composed of a pattern of five diamonds, an Indigenous symbol; the other 20 stars are white, as Mississippi was the 20th state to join the Union. The flag was adopted on January 11, 2021.

Concept note-5: -The 17 stars in the flag recall that Ohio was the 17th state to join the Union. The shape of the flag is sometimes referred to as a burgee, which is properly a nautical term.
