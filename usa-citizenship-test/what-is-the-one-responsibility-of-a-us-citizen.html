What is the one responsibility of a U.S. Citizen?

(A) ** serve on a jury

(B) become a firefighter

(C) join the military

(D) run for office

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The most important function of a juror is to listen to all evidence presented at trial, and to decide the facts of the case. The Judge is there to determine the legal aspects of the case and to keep the trial moving forward. The performance of jury service is the fulfillment of a civic obligation.

Concept note-2: -Citizens have a responsibility to participate in the political process by registering and voting in elections. Serving on a jury is another responsibility of citizenship.

Concept note-3: -Jury service is one responsibility that is only for a United States citizen.

Concept note-4: -Rights for United States Citizens Only The right to vote. The right to run for elected offices such as president or vice president. The right to apply for federal employment and for the federal government. The right to obtain a United States passport.

Concept note-5: -They must vote, pay taxes, and participate in the draft. What are the three duties expected of US citizens? Voting is the most important duty because it is our responsibility to let people who are right to represent us in the government.
