The idea of self government is in three words, what are the words?

(A) ** We the people

(B) I don’t know

(C) I don’t care

(D) This is dumb

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The idea of self-government is in the first three words of the Constitution. What are these words? The first three words of the Constitution are “We the People.” The document says that the people of the United States choose to create the government.

Concept note-2: -“We the People” includes all the citizens of the United States of America. The importance of this phrase shows that it wasn’t just the framers of the Constitution or the legislators who were given powers to the government. Instead, the government gets all of its powers from the Citizens of the United States of America.

Concept note-3: -Written in 1787, ratified in 1788, and in operation since 1789, the United States Constitution is the world’s longest surviving written charter of government. Its first three words – “We The People” – affirm that the government of the United States exists to serve its citizens.

Concept note-4: -Self-government. The people have the exclusive right of governing themselves as a free, sovereign, and independent state.
