[SOURCES]
If you did not want to rebel and boycott Great Britain and still wanted to be under the control of the King you were labeled a

(A) Patriot

(B) ** Loyalist

(C) American

(D) Militia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Loyalists, often called Tories, were loyal to the crown for several reasons. They were mostly upper class and lived in cities and wanted to keep their wealth and land. Many had valuable ties with the British or jobs in the royal government.

Concept note-2: -Loyalists wanted to pursue peaceful forms of protest because they believed that violence would give rise to mob rule or tyranny. They also believed that independence would mean the loss of economic benefits derived from membership in the British mercantile system.

Concept note-3: -Loyalists were American colonists, of different ethnic backgrounds, who supported the British cause during the American Revolutionary War (1775–83). Tens of thousands of Loyalists migrated to British North America during and after the war.

Concept note-4: -Vocabulary: • Loyalist-a colonist who supported the crown/king of England • Patriot-a colonist who rejected British rule over the colonies during the American Revolution Activity: 1.
